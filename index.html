Notandia adds transparent publisher context and formal post-publication signals to scholarly literature. It exposes evidence, chronology, provenance, and coverage instead of assigning opaque quality scores.

Released, tested, and planned capabilities remain explicit.

Formal article events

Opt-in DOI checks expose retractions, concerns, corrections, reinstatements, withdrawals, removals, and duplicate-publication findings with provenance and chronology.

Roadmap

Broader contextual coverage

The architecture supports Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, and Zotero while keeping article events separate from journal or publisher context.

Work-level taxonomy

Color supports the meaning; it never replaces the label.

Formal publication events require different semantics. A correction is not a retraction, and a reinstatement changes the current state without erasing the historical timeline.

×
Retracted

A formal retraction affects the work.

!
Expression of concern

Serious concerns are formally recorded but remain unresolved.

✎
Corrected

A correction, corrigendum, or erratum should be consulted.

↩
Reinstated

A prior retraction was reversed; the history remains visible.

≡
Duplicate-publication finding

Substantial overlap or duplication is documented as a finding or reason.

–
Withdrawn or removed

A version was withdrawn or removed from publication.

Product principles

Evidence before verdicts.

Scope is explicit

Article events, journal context, publisher context, and identity warnings remain separate.

Coverage is visible

Checked, unresolved, failed, and deferred identifiers are reported instead of silently treated as clear.

Provenance is inspectable

Signals expose source, identifier, event chronology, retrieval date, and applicable exceptions.

Trust boundaries

Open source. No product analytics. No hidden score.
